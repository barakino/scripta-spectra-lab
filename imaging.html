The instruments

Imaging Lab

The Scripta/Spectra Lab operates a growing suite of imaging and motion-capture instruments for documenting inscriptions and studying the act of writing itself. We are glad to image inscriptions for colleagues and institutions — use the form below to arrange a visit.

What we have installed

Hyperspectral camera (VIS–NIR)

A hyperspectral imaging camera covering the visible to near-infrared range, capturing a full spectrum at every pixel — used to enhance the legibility of faded or invisible ink and to characterize writing materials from a single scan.

Multispectral high-resolution camera

A high-resolution multispectral camera for detailed, portable documentation of ostraca, papyri, and other inscribed surfaces across selected spectral bands.

PenCapture handwriting recording

Software to record handwriting kinematics during controlled writing experiments — capturing the dynamics of the hand (speed, pressure, timing) that underlie our models of scribal movement and script evolution. Get Pen Capture on the App Store →

Natural-writing surface for ostraca & papyri in development

We are developing a recording surface that lets scribes write on ostraca and papyri in a natural way while capturing the characteristics of the writing act — bridging modern motion capture and ancient writing media.

Scripta/Spectra Lab

The Scripta/Spectra Lab develops mathematics, statistics, and imaging for the study of human culture — reading ancient scripts, tracing how writing evolves, and recovering what the eye can no longer see — while letting the hard problems of humanistic data reshape the mathematics itself.
